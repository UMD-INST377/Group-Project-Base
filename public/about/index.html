About

The problem we are trying to solve is the lack of data visualization when interacting with NBA data. The information in our database can be found in dozens of locations across the internet, but they are all text-heavy tables of data that the user must sort through.

The database we chose is from a group memberâ€™s INST 327 project. The database has information about NBA teams, arenas, awards, stats, games, and more. We chose this database for a couple of reasons. The first reason is that it is topical. The NBA season is just around the corner and so this modification to this dataset will excite those who care about the sport and attract others as well. The second reason is that the database has a lot of data. The data consist of data from 2016 through the 2020 season. We believe that with all of the different data, we can reach many audiences with this database.

Some of the many audiences that we hope to reach with our project are fans who are interested in learning more about the NBA, gamblers/staff involved with teams, or those who perform predictions for monetary gain. Young fans who want a roster presentation with more images may also benefit from our project. Anyone who is interested in historical NBA data that does not want to interact with a text-heavy table of data should benefit from our project.

We plan to solve the problems we have identified by providing a less text-heavy database format for younger fans, focusing more on visuals than tables and data. We will effectively provide data on teams, arenas, and active players, supplementing user knowledge and establishing a means to gather and analyze data on the NBA.
